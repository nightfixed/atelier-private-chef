The story

Where the name comes from

This name was not chosen from a branding dictionary. It was inherited.

Before twenty years of craft, before Germany, England, French technique and Japanese fermentation — I was my mother’s helper.

I stood beside her in the kitchen. She explained every step, let me do it, corrected me when needed. Every time, the same three things.

That was all.

“Asezat” is not a brand. It is a promise.

We didn’t start with a product list. We started with recipes cooked dozens of times, until they were exactly right. A Romanian base — pork lard, crackling, rendered fat — with ingredients brought from places that know how to do things well: paprika from Hungary, harissa from Tunisia, soy sauce from Japan, salt from the Himalayas. Not blending for the sake of blending. Curiosity with roots.

Cluj Lard Spread
Seasoned pork lard

My grandmother made it in November, after slaughter. The smell filled the whole house. This is that same lard — slowly rendered, unhurried, with roasted garlic that leaves its sweetness in the fat, mountain thyme and smoked paprika from Hungary. Spread it on a slice of dark bread and you remember something.

Clarified Smoked Lard
Clarified & smoked

Clarification is pure patience — hours at low heat until the fat turns golden and translucent. What’s left is only what matters. The smoke went in before the jar was sealed — not too much, just enough to know it’s there. Good for anything that calls for a layer of flavour without noise.

Spiced Crackling Paste
Spreadable paste

Crackling is already something. We ground it fine, brought it together with Tunisian harissa, roasted garlic, a little soy sauce and a spoonful of local honey. Everything went briefly into 180°C — not to cook, but to bind. What comes out doesn’t resemble anything you can buy in a shop. That’s the intention.

It’s not just carefully chosen ingredients. It’s the soul of what ASEZAT stands for and the way a recipe is built — which makes the final product taste like something your grandmother made, reinterpreted for today.

Philosophy

Three Principles

01
Don’t rush.

Fermentation takes weeks. Ageing takes months. Patience is not a lack — it is an ingredient. Every jar is ready when it is ready, not when it is convenient.

02
Do everything well.

pH controlled. Salt weighed to the gram. Lot numbered. No step skipped, no compromise hidden behind a label.

03
Put soul into everything.

Without this, the first two are just technique. With it, they become craft. An ASEZAT jar is not a product — it is the decision of someone who refused to rush.

What you want to know

For restaurants and HoReCa, the minimum is 12 jars per product. For retail and delicatessen, 24 jars per reference. For individual or gift orders, quantities are more flexible — write to us and we will figure it out.

Shelf life is 12 months from the production date, stored at room temperature away from direct light. Once opened, keep refrigerated and consume within 3–5 days.

Currently we deliver primarily within Transylvania and Cluj-Napoca. For larger orders or partners in other regions, delivery can be arranged — we discuss based on volume. International export is available for MOQ quantities with full EU documentation.

Yes, we offer custom labelling for B2B partners at volume orders. You can choose to show your brand, the ASEZAT brand, or both. We discuss details after the first pilot order.

Production takes place in an EU certified facility, with a valid oval stamp, declaration of conformity and full lot traceability. All documentation required for HoReCa or export is available on request.
